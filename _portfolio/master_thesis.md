excerpt: "The task of visual super-resolution aims at mapping low-resolution visual data to its corresponding high-resolution representation. This thesis mainly focuses on two such tasks of visual super-resolution, namely, image super-resolution and video super-resolution. In this do- main, Generative models like Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), Variational AutoEncoders (VAEs), and Normalizing Flows have shown excellent ability to solve this task. One of the critical aspects of ob- taining high-fidelity super-resolution results is to encode long-range dependencies in the network. To capture long-term dependencies with previous methods, we must utilize all the features. However, we know that all of the features are not equally important, and we must use critical features for efficient modeling of long-term dependencies. To that end, the attention mechanism in the neural network is used widely. The goal of this thesis is to study whether attention mechanism helps generative models. With this goal in mind, we first study the effect of adding attention along different dimensions in a GAN-like model. Further, an efficient attention cell is proposed using Neural Architec- ture Search (NAS) to search for the most optimal way to introduce attention in GANs for super-resolution. Next, to model long-range dependencies in normalizing flows, we introduce two types of attention mechanisms, i.e., invertible map-based and scaled dot-product atten- tion, into conditional generative flow models. The newly proposed attention mechanism models the global data dependencies, leading to more comprehensive flow models for super-resolution. The evaluation on multiple super-resolution tasks demonstrates that the introduced attention mechanisms for both GAN- and flow-based generative models are effective. The methods proposed in this thesis can provide better or comparable results than those of the existing state-of-the-art methods with much lighter model, and significantly better train and inference time performance.
